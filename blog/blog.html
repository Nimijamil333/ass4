Efforts to Bring-up Change

Save the Children has been working in Pakistan for more than 30 years, helping children and families in the aftermath of disasters and living in crisis. In 2009-2010, conflict forced more than 2 million people from their homes. Save the Children was on the ground distributing lifesaving and life-sustaining supplies including shelter kits, food, education and medical aid.Pakistan is no stranger to struggles: an earthquake in 2005. Monsoon flooding in 2010. Decades-long conflict. Save the Children has been on the ground to help – keeping children healthy, educated and safe.

Although these organizations support orphans in Pakistan and children in need by providing aid to Pakistan and, in many cases, providing resources directly to children, those who do not have access to the resources from foreign aid are forced to find alternate ways to support themselves.
